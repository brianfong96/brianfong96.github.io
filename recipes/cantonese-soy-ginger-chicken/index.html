Recipe

Cantonese-Style Soy & Ginger Baked Chicken

Click any section to expand. Click again to collapse.

Back to Recipes

Overview

click to explore

A rich, savory-sweet roast chicken utilizing the classic Cantonese marinade trinity: light soy for salt, dark soy for deep mahogany color, and Shaoxing wine for aroma. Optimized for 2kg of bone-in dark meat with a crispy, lacquered skin and succulent interior.

10 Items

Ingredients

click to explore
  • 2 kg Mixed Drumsticks & Bone-in Thighs
  • 6 tbsp Light Soy Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Dark Soy Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Oyster Sauce
  • 3 tbsp Cooking Wine (Shaoxing)
  • 3 tbsp Honey
  • 2 tbsp Oil (Vegetable or Sesame)
  • 6 cloves Garlic (minced)
  • 2-inch knob Ginger (grated or finely minced)
  • Optional: 1/2 tsp White Pepper or Five-Spice Powder
4 Steps

Marinade Instructions

click to explore
  1. Prep: Pat chicken pieces completely dry with paper towels. Use a fork or knife tip to prick the skin; this helps the marinade penetrate the thick muscle of the thighs.
  2. Mix: In a large bowl, whisk the light soy, dark soy, oyster sauce, cooking wine, honey, oil, garlic, and ginger until the honey is fully dissolved.
  3. Combine: Add the chicken to the bowl (or a heavy-duty gallon freezer bag). Massage the marinade into the meat, ensuring it gets under the skin where possible.
  4. Chill: Refrigerate for at least 4 hours. For best depth of flavor and color, marinate for 12–18 hours. Flip the bag or stir halfway through.
Oven

Baking

click to explore
Oven 175°C / 350°F
Time 35–45 min
Target 79–82°C / 175–180°F Fall-off-the-bone tender
Glaze Brush at 20 min With remaining marinade

Line a baking sheet with foil and use a wire rack for air circulation. Arrange chicken skin-side up with space between pieces. If skin isn't deeply browned by 40 min, broil 2–3 minutes — watch closely as honey and dark soy can burn quickly.

Tips

Key Tips

click to explore
  • Always pat the chicken dry before marinating — excess water dilutes the soy sauces and prevents the skin from crisping.
  • Don't skip the oil in the marinade; it acts as a heat conductor to help render the chicken fat and brown the skin evenly.
  • For thighs and drumsticks, aim for 79–82°C (175–180°F) internal — the higher temp breaks down connective tissue for "fall-off-the-bone" tender meat.
  • Let the chicken rest 5–10 minutes before serving to allow juices to redistribute.
