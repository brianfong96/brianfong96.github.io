# Cantonese-Style Soy & Ginger Baked Chicken

**Summary:** A rich, savory-sweet roast chicken recipe utilizing the classic "holy trinity" of Cantonese marinade: light soy for salt, dark soy for deep mahogany color, and Shaoxing wine for aroma. This recipe is optimized for 2kg of bone-in dark meat, ensuring a crispy, lacquered skin and succulent interior.

### Ingredients
* **Chicken:** 2kg mixed drumsticks and bone-in thighs
* **Light Soy Sauce:** 6 tbsp
* **Dark Soy Sauce:** 2 tbsp
* **Oyster Sauce:** 2 tbsp
* **Cooking Wine (Shaoxing):** 3 tbsp
* **Honey:** 3 tbsp
* **Oil (Vegetable or Sesame):** 2 tbsp
* **Garlic:** 6 cloves, minced
* **Ginger:** 2-inch knob, grated or finely minced
* **Optional:** 1/2 tsp white pepper or five-spice powder

---

### Marinade Instructions
1. **Prep the Chicken:** Pat the chicken pieces completely dry with paper towels. Use a fork or tip of a knife to prick the skin a few times; this helps the marinade penetrate the thick muscle of the thighs.
2. **Mix:** In a large bowl, whisk the light soy, dark soy, oyster sauce, cooking wine, honey, oil, garlic, and ginger until the honey is fully dissolved.
3. **Combine:** Add the chicken to the bowl (or a heavy-duty gallon freezer bag). Massage the marinade into the meat, ensuring it gets under the skin where possible.
4. **Chill:** Place in the refrigerator for **at least 4 hours**. For the best depth of flavor and color, marinate for **12 to 18 hours**. Flip the bag or stir the bowl halfway through to ensure even coloring from the dark soy.

---

### Oven Cooking Instructions
1. **Preheat:** Set your oven to **175°C (350°F)**.
2. **Arrange:** Line a large baking sheet with foil and place a wire rack on top (optional, but helps air circulation). Arrange chicken skin-side up with space between pieces.
3. **Bake:**
   * **Total Time:** 35–45 minutes.
   * **The Glaze:** Halfway through (around 20 minutes), brush the tops with any remaining marinade from the bowl.
4. **Crisp:** If the skin isn't deeply browned by the 40-minute mark, turn on the broiler for 2–3 minutes. Watch closely as the honey and dark soy can burn quickly.

---

### Key Targets & Tips
* **Internal Temperature:** The chicken is done at 74°C (165°F), but for thighs and drumsticks, aim for **79°C–82°C (175°F–180°F)**. This higher temp breaks down the connective tissue, making the meat "fall-off-the-bone" tender.
* **The "Dry" Secret:** Always pat the chicken dry before marinating. Excess water dilutes the soy sauces and prevents the skin from crisping.
* **Oil in Marinade:** Don't skip the oil in the marinade; it acts as a heat conductor in the oven to help render the chicken fat and brown the skin evenly.
* **Resting:** Let the chicken rest for 5–10 minutes before serving to allow the juices to redistribute.
