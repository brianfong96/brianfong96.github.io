Will AI Take Over?

March 22, 2026 Back to Blog

I was asked a question today that actually made me pause. It wasn't about whether AI will disrupt the job market, but whether it will become a new governing system, a digital Skynet, taking over humanity.

Honestly, I didn't have an answer. In this era, the tech moves too fast to be certain about anything. I remember my manager mentioning GPT-3 back in December 2022. I was skeptical, but even then, I was blown away. I didn't think it would challenge Google or become a "source of truth" because of the hallucinations. I didn't think it would be a "revolution," as my dad called it.

I was very wrong.

In the years since, it has seeped into every facet of society. It feels like it can do almost anything given enough time. Its tendrils reach into our daily habits, and I've felt a shift in my own mind. I'm perhaps not as sharp on the technical side as I used to be; I can't compete with a machine's speed. But critical thinking has never felt more vital. It's incredibly tempting to offload the heavy lifting of the mind and let AI take the wheel. As the tools improve, that temptation only gets stronger.

That is exactly why I'm blogging again. I need to make my brain work. I need to feel like me. I'm not claiming human creativity will always supersede AI, but I want to cultivate my own mind to see how far it can go. AI is a brilliant tool for achievement, but a poor substitute for existence.

Which brings me back to the original question: Will AI take over?

Governments will certainly leverage it, but I don't think AI will "rule" us. Not because of a sudden surge in human potential, but because of human ego. The people currently in power will use every tool available to stay there, but they won't surrender that power to the tools themselves. We are a greedy species. It won't be easy clawing the throne from our hands.
